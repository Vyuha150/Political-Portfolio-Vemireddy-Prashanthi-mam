Our Alliance for welfare of Andhra Pradesh

The alliance of the Telugu Desam Party (TDP), Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), and Jana Sena Party (JSP) for the 2024 elections in Andhra Pradesh is a strategic move aimed at promoting the welfare of the state. This coalition seeks to leverage the strengths of each party—TDP's established regional presence, BJP's national influence, and JSP's appeal among the youth and various communities—to work collectively for the development and betterment of Andhra Pradesh.

Jana Sena Party

The Jana Sena Party is an Indian regional political party in the states of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. It was founded by the actor-politician Pawan Kalyan at the International Convention Centre in Hyderabad on 14 March 2014.

Telugu Desam Party

The Telugu Desam Party is an Indian Telugu regionalist political party with influence in the states of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.[15] It was founded by the former chief minister of Andhra Pradesh N. T. Rama Rao (N.T.R.) on 29 March 1982.

Bharatiya Janata Party

The Bharatiya Janata Party is a political party in India and one of the two major Indian political parties alongside the Indian National Congress.[35] Since 2014, it has been the ruling political party in India under the incumbent Prime Minister Narendra Modi.
